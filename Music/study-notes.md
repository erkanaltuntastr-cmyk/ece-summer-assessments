## Priority Topics

### 1. African Music

Key ideas:
- African music is often passed on by ear. This is called aural tradition.
- A master drummer leads the ensemble.
- Call and response is a musical conversation where one part plays or sings and another replies.
- Polyrhythm means two or more different rhythms are played at the same time.
- Improvisation means making up music on the spot.
- African music is often social and linked to community events, celebration, dance, singing and participation.

Key instruments:
- Djembe: goblet-shaped drum.
- Talking drum: drum that can change pitch by tightening or loosening cords.
- Balafon: wooden xylophone.
- Kora: string instrument.
- Shekere: shaker.
- Marimba: wooden percussion instrument.

### 2. Keyboard and Notation

Key ideas:
- A stave has five lines.
- The treble clef is used for higher notes.
- Notes in the spaces of the treble clef spell FACE.
- Notes on the lines can be remembered as Every Good Boy Deserves Football.
- Bars are divided by barlines.
- A double barline shows the end.
- Pitch means how high or low a note is.
- Melody means the tune.

Keyboard reminders:
- Finger 1 is the thumb.
- Finger 5 is the little finger.
- Good hand position helps fluency and control.

### 3. Rhythm and Note Values

Key ideas:
- Pulse is the steady beat.
- Rhythm is the pattern of long and short notes over the pulse.
- Crotchet = 1 beat.
- Minim = 2 beats.
- Semibreve = 4 beats.
- Quaver = half a beat.

### 4. Elements of Music

Structure:
- How a piece of music is organised.
- Repeated sections and patterns help create structure.

Tempo:
- The speed of the music.
- Presto = very fast.
- Allegro = fast.
- Moderato = moderate speed.
- Lento = slow.
- Accelerando = gradually getting faster.
- Rallentando = gradually getting slower.

Dynamics:
- The volume of the music.
- Piano = quiet.
- Forte = loud.

Texture:
- How many layers of sound there are and how they fit together.
- Unison = everyone performs the same thing at the same time.
- Polyrhythm = several rhythms layered together.

Pitch:
- How high or low a note is.
- Ascending = going up.
- Descending = going down.
- Conjunct = moving by step to neighbouring notes.
- Disjunct = moving by leap.

Articulation:
- How notes are attacked and released.
- Staccato = short and detached.
- Legato = smooth and joined.

Metre:
- How beats are grouped in bars.
- Time signatures show how many beats are in a bar.

### 5. Compose a Melody

What makes a good melody:
- It has a clear shape.
- It uses repeated ideas or patterns.
- It balances stepwise movement and leaps.
- It fits the rhythm and metre.
- It has a clear ending.

Instrumental families to revise:
- Strings
- Woodwind
- Brass
- Percussion
